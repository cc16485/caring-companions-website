Overnight Care

Someone present through the night.

For families whose biggest worry is the hours between dinner and breakfast, a caregiver stays overnight, typically across multi-day scheduled shifts.

Start the Care Assessment Ask Cara if this fits

What is Overnight Care?

A caregiver comes to the home in the evening and stays through the night, typically an 8–12 hour shift from evening to morning, so someone is awake and ready to help with bathroom trips, wandering, medications, or anything else that happens after dark. It sits between Hourly Care and full Live-In or 24-Hour Care, ideal when nighttime safety is the main concern but round-the-clock daytime supervision isn't yet needed.

Who it's for

✓  Families most worried about nighttime falls or wandering

✓  Those who manage daytime care themselves but need overnight relief

✓  People recently discharged who need overnight monitoring temporarily

✓  Anyone comparing Overnight against Live-In or 24-Hour Care

Overnight Care is a nightly shift: the caregiver arrives in the evening, covers the night, and leaves in the morning, so you only pay for the nighttime hours you actually need.
