Local Directory

Compiled as a community service and updated periodically. Polk County listings were gathered from public sources, please verify hours, phone numbers, and eligibility directly with each organization before relying on them.

Citizens Memorial Hospital (CMH)

1500 N. Oakland Ave, Bolivar, MO 65613 · (417) 326-6000 · citizensmemorial.com. Polk County's community hospital and health system, with clinics, emergency care, and rehabilitation services.

Use this when: there's a medical need or emergency, or a hospital stay is ending and discharge planning is starting.

Citizens Memorial Home Care & Hospice

Part of the CMH health system, Bolivar · (417) 326-6000. Skilled home health and hospice services ordered by a physician, confirm current services and referral eligibility directly.

Use this when: a doctor has ordered skilled nursing or therapy at home, or a family is shifting toward comfort-focused care.

Citizens Memorial Healthcare Facility

1218 W. Locust St, Bolivar, MO 65613 · (417) 326-7648. A 111-bed skilled nursing facility offering short-term rehabilitation, long-term nursing care, and assisted living. Confirm current Medicaid/Medicare acceptance and openings directly.

Use this when: a doctor has ordered skilled nursing care, or long-term custodial or assisted-living care is needed.

Parkview Health Care Facility

Bolivar, MO (part of the Citizens Memorial family), skilled nursing and long-term care. Confirm current address, phone, Medicaid/Medicare acceptance, and openings directly.

Use this when: you're comparing skilled nursing or long-term care options in the Bolivar area.

Polk County Senior Center

1850 W. Broadway St, Bolivar, MO 65613 · (417) 326-5570. A SeniorAge center and the hub for senior services in Polk County: congregate and home-delivered meals, Medicare counseling, benefits screening, case management, a respite-care registry, homemaker help, exercise classes, and warming/cooling center access.

Use this when: you're not sure where to start, or need meals, benefits help, or social connection close to home.

OATS Transit, Southwest Region

(417) 887-9272 · oatstransit.org. Shared-ride transportation for medical appointments, shopping, and errands, plus free rides to the senior center's nutrition program. Advance reservations generally required.

Use this when: your loved one no longer drives but needs rides to appointments, shopping, or the senior center.

SeniorAge Area Agency on Aging

(417) 862-0762 · toll-free (800) 497-0822 · senioragemo.org. Serves Polk and 16 surrounding Southwest Missouri counties with meals, transportation, benefits counseling, case management, and caregiver support.

Use this when: you want one starting point for public programs before you know exactly what you need.

Missouri Senior Resource Line

1-800-235-5503. Statewide DHSS line connecting callers to their Area Agency on Aging for meals, transportation, caregiver support, legal aid, home modifications, and Ombudsman services.

Use this when: you don't know where to start and want one number to call.

Missouri Medicaid HCBS (Aged & Disabled Waiver / CDS)

Home and Community-Based Services waivers can make in-home care free for those who qualify. Caring Companions helps Polk County families apply for both the Aged & Disabled Waiver and Independent Living Waiver / Consumer Directed Services, at no cost, (417) 234-8494.

Use this when: private pay isn't sustainable long-term and income/asset limits may qualify your loved one.

Missouri Veterans Commission, Veterans Service Officer

Find your assigned Veterans Service Officer with the official locator at mvc.dps.mo.gov. The regional Springfield office, (417) 895-6532, serves the surrounding area. VSOs help veterans and qualifying family members apply for state and federal benefits, including Aid & Attendance.

Use this when: a veteran or surviving spouse may qualify for VA health, pension, or Aid & Attendance benefits.

💡 What we want every Polk County family to know

The Polk County Senior Center is a great first call.

The team in Bolivar can explain meals, benefits, and many of the programs available to older adults and caregivers, (417) 326-5570.

Hospital discharge doesn't automatically include home care.

If your loved one needs ongoing help at home after a CMH stay, ask the discharge planner whether home health, private-duty home care, or both are appropriate.

Most families increase care gradually.

Many begin with help during the busiest parts of the day and adjust as needs change, you don't have to decide everything at once.
