The journey

Nine modules, one road.

Start at the beginning or jump to tonight’s problem. Each module stands on its own, and they’re better together.

1 Available now · ~20 min

You Are Not Failing

The feelings nobody warns you about: grief that starts early, guilt, anger, and the love that holds it all.

 ▶ 2 Available now · ~25 min

Understanding the Changes in the Brain

They are not giving you a hard time. They are having a hard time. The detective questions, and the one firm rule about sudden confusion.

 ▶ 3 Available now · ~25 min

Stop Arguing With Dementia

Connection is often more helpful than correction. The feeling beneath the words, the validate-then-redirect sequence, and the phrases that open doors.

 ▶ 4 Available now · ~27 min

When Words and Behaviors Hurt

Accusations, anger, and the night they don’t know your face. The hurt you feel is real, and there are ways through, for both of you.

 ▶ 5 Available now · ~28 min

Communication That Creates Calm

Approach, pace, choices, and dignity, including Teepa Snow’s Positive Physical Approach™, the same craft our professional caregivers train in.

 ▶ 6 Available now · ~31 min

The Stories That Still Exist

A person is more than what they can remember today. The beautiful road: identity, music, story doors, and the “This Is Who I Am” keepsake you’ll build.

 ▶ 7 Available now · ~27 min

Daily Care Without the Battle

Not a nursing course, a dignity course: bathing, dressing, the bathroom, and meals, with Teepa Snow’s Hand-under-Hand™.

 ▶ 8 Available now · ~27 min

Caregiver Burnout and Accepting Help

The module about you. No bubble baths: specific asks, the family meeting, your circle of support, and when one pair of hands is no longer enough.

 ▶ 9 Available now · ~30 min

Hard Decisions, Safety, and What Comes Next

Driving, living alone, wandering, the paperwork, and what comes next. Nobody here tells you what to decide, the compass stays in your hands.

 ▶ ❤ The ending

Love Still Lives Here

Not a lesson. The last words of the journey, a montage of every family, and a certificate with your name on it.

The teaching behind the course

This course draws on widely accepted person-centered dementia care, including the teachings of dementia educator Teepa Snow and her Positive Approach to Care®, which Caring Companions is licensed to use and teaches to every professional caregiver on our own team. Families who want to go deeper can explore her demonstrations and resources at teepasnow.com.

If you need help right now

Emergencies: 911 · Emotional crisis: call or text 988 · Alzheimer’s Association 24/7 Helpline: (800) 272-3900 · Sudden changes: call their provider. · Local help: SW Missouri Caregiver Resource Directory.
