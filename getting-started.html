What is home care?

Home care means a trusted caregiver comes to the home to help a senior or disabled adult with daily tasks, so they can keep living safely and comfortably in the place they love. The level of care depends on the person's needs — it can range from a few hours a day or a few days a week, all the way to 24-hour or live-in care.

When is home care helpful?

Home care can make a real difference in the well-being and quality of life of a senior or disabled adult — and their family. Some of the most common situations:

✓ A loved one needs some help to live safely on their own
✓ Mild cognitive impairment (MCI) or more serious dementia
✓ For companionship and healthier social connection
✓ Following a serious medical incident or hospitalization
✓ A chronic or age-related health condition or illness
✓ A family caregiver needs support or time to rest and recharge
