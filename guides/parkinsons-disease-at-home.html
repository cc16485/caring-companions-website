Parkinson's Disease at Home

Reviewed by a Caring Companions Care Coordinator · Last updated July 2026 · About 5 minute read

Parkinson's affects movement, balance, and eventually speech and swallowing. Support looks different at each stage, and fall risk is often the most urgent early concern.

Mobility and fall risk

Tremors, stiffness, and "freezing" episodes make falls a leading safety concern. Clear walkways, grab bars, and physical therapy focused on gait and balance all reduce risk meaningfully - see our Fall Prevention guide for a full home walkthrough.

Source: Parkinson's Foundation, Fall Prevention.

Daily support that helps

Medication timing matters enormously with Parkinson's - doses are often scheduled precisely and missing a window can affect mobility for hours. Assistance with dressing, transfers, and meals (chewing/swallowing difficulty is common in later stages) is often needed even when cognition is unaffected.

As the condition progresses

Care needs typically increase gradually rather than suddenly, which makes a flexible care plan valuable — starting with a few hours of help and adding hands-on personal care or overnight support as balance and mobility change.
